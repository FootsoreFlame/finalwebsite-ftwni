having just me myself and my thoughts for the whole day has made me turn to other forms of comfort. primarily, going online and music. i will talk about the internet another time, however for now, i will focus on the impact music has had on my life. to start, i think that music is something very influential and has the power to bring people together. it can induce feeling, emotions, make you feel things. a tool used by therapists to help their clients is called the iso principle. this tool is used to induce feelings to a client through music. to use the iso principle, you first start with something calming, more chill, typically ambient or slow music. then, you gradually switch to something more upbeat. if you apply this logic to yourself, you can see how playing the same music that you pair with anger or sadness over and over can thus make you feel those emotions. i personally use this method to increase my productivity and attentiveness whenever i study. i often use soft and melodic music (typically lofi or alternative) in the background whenever i study to help me focus. another way to use this principle effectively that i personally don't use but i know works is to music playlist that gradually moves from uncomfortable emotions to comfortable ones. this can be a healthy coping strategy and facilitate a positive mood change. another aspect of music is how it can be connected to certain times or places in your life. for example, lets say you get into a relationship. on your first date, you guys play this one song. from that point on, whenever you hear that song, you will remember that moment of you two, together, happy. however, all good things come to an end. you two end up breaking up and from that point onwards, that song will only remind you of loss and regret. this song can have both a negative and positive feeling attached to it depending on what significance is related to that song. music has the remarkable ability to serve as a time capsule, preserving the emotions and memories of specific moments in our lives. it becomes a soundtrack to our lives, the connection music and our memories is so potent that a single song can transport us back to a particular time, evoking the same emotions we felt in that moment. i find music to bring me away from the reality of stress and anxiety that i often put myself through. it helps me relax and calm down to work better. i also find music to be an important bond with people. sharing music taste with people i like is something i can talk about and having shared interests is something i find important when looking for relationships, either friendly or intimate. i feel comfortable sharing songs i like with others and they should feel the same. music has the power to connect people and share emotional connections with them.

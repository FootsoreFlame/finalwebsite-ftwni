personally, i see expressing gratitude as one of the main principles to establishing a positive and harmonious connection with others. when we express gratitude, we acknowledge the efforts, kindness, or support provided by someone else. this not only fosters a sense of appreciation but also strengthens the bonds between individuals. gratitude creates a positive atmosphere, promotes empathy, and encourages a culture of mutual respect. it serves as a foundation for building meaningful relationships, whether in personal or professional settings. another benefit of expressing gratitude is that it will quite literally make you a better person, in all aspects. not only will you feel happier and gain a more positive view on life, your emotional and mental health will increase. as well as this, you will become a better student. according to an article by harvard business school, it was discovered that using journaling can increase your productivity by up to 22.8%. journaling is something super simple to get into the habit of doing and with those type of results there is little reason to not want to. of course, just because i tell you doesn't mean you will. if we are being honest with ourselves, you will forget all about this website in 10 minutes time. this advice is only for those who need it. those who want it. those who are willing to sacrifice and do the hard things right now so that their future are brighter. what is scrolling for 2 hours on instagram going to do for your future? journaling is an important tool that you can use in order for you to write down and organize your thoughts. you can also use this tool to, you guessed it, express gratitude for things. i began journaling in late december of 2023. i think i initially started gratitude journaling in order to heal myself emotionally. during this time i was confused. clouded by thoughts. i used journaling as a healthy outlet to express my thoughts. i have been using this tool ever since as a way to lay out my thoughts. i have written all sorts of things inside of this journal. goals. questions. reflecting on life. i find it easier to plan out my goals by writing them down. this way, there is no what ifs or hoping for something to happen. everything i plan to do is imperative to accomplishing my goal. after about a week of staying consistent with this habit, i noticed a stark contrast from how i was feeling and how others saw me. i felt an almost immediate relief from my worries and problems. others also noted how i looked happier and more positive, an aspect of myself that i try to influence onto others.
